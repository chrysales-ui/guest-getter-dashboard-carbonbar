What happened: Carbon Bar spent $2,066 across Google and Meta this period (Meta $1,222 + Google $844). Google drove 48 reservations at $17.58 each — guests spend $217.26 per reservation on average ($72.71/cover × 2.99 avg party size, Experience reservations only). Meta captured 347 leads across its campaigns. Of those, 12 reservations were created after a lead form was submitted (11 unique people — one person booked twice across two campaigns). 6 completed their visit spending $1,570.70 total, 3 are upcoming, and 3 cancelled. Blended cost per reservation: $34.43. The big story: the Discover campaign cut cost per lead from $8.85 to $1.85 — a 79% improvement.

What's Next
Google: Increase PMAX budget
Reservations are up, cost per reservation is down to $17.58, and spend per reservation is $217.26. All 4 campaigns are budget-limited — there's clear headroom to scale without sacrificing efficiency.
Meta: Follow-up sequence for unbooked leads
335 leads filled out the form but haven't made a reservation yet. An SMS or email sequence in the days after form submission could move a meaningful portion into bookings. Re-run cross-match in 30 days.
Meta: Tighten audience exclusions
Exclude existing reservation database contacts from the Discover campaign so spend reaches genuinely new audiences and drives more net-new guests.
Track 90-day return rates
With guests acquired this period, we now have a cohort to watch. In 90 days, we'll report what percentage came back — that's the retention story for Carbon Bar.
What we can't measure yet
Per-cover spend of $72.71 (= $217.26/reservation at 2.99 avg party size) reflects Experience reservations only (506 of 570 completed visits). 64 non-Experience (à la carte) reservations have $0 in the tracking system — their actual spend is not captured. A POS integration would unlock true per-cover spend across all diners. 335 of 347 Meta leads have no reservation on file yet.
